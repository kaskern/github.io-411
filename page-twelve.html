Back Into Town

Her dog kept barking and barking and pulling on her cloak to lead her down the steps and back to town. Something was not right. Her father never was without their dog especially this well into the night. She decided to let the dog lead her all the way back into town. Wherever he took her she knw it would not be good. When they made it into town the only life that surrounded them was the flickering lanterns. Not a sound. The wind was dead, and yet, there again that awful smell. Her dog began to growl inching close and then not too far away from her and into a pitch black alleyway that was colder and darker and almost as lifeless as space. She inched closer and closer.
