Speaking Clearly

Before she could question the weather and while sitting on her usual pew in the front entrance, she heard her grandfather holler. It was not his usual holler, but one of confidence and grace, as if he was a younger version of himself able to speak and be coherent. She decided to follow. First the weather, the smell, now this? How weird could this night get? He never spoke to her only in indecipherable mumblings and shouts and nudges. Did she know where to go? She had never set foot in the temple before and never prayed to the spirits or the myth. Should she have? She set foot to where her grandfather kneeled.
