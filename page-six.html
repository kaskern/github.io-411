Her Friday Night

She started to notice that something was weird in the air. She could tell when the seasons were about to change, but the smell in the air was peculiar. She thought maybe the old church, in which she waited for her grandfather, was the culprit and decided to keep cleaning her pocketknife. The old man was a fool to invite her here every Friday, but she did it because she often felt sorry for his loss of her mother and decided to please the old man with being in the same presence; her mother would have. Until she heard the wind pick up, and the clouds roll in, and the harbor start to rock violently.
